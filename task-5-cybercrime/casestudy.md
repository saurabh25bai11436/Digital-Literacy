 ### **Case Study: Cyberbullying and Doxing in Campus Communities**

[cite_start]**What the crime is:**  Cyberbullying is a pervasive digital crime that involves using electronic communication platforms—such as social media networks, messaging applications, or online gaming forums—to intentionally harass, threaten, demean, or humiliate a targeted individual on a repeated basis. Unlike traditional bullying, it can happen around the clock and reach a massive audience instantly.

[cite_start]**How it typically happens (step-by-step):**
1. **The Trigger:** A minor, seemingly harmless disagreement occurs between students during a heated debate in a college WhatsApp group.
2. **The Escalation:** A coordinated group begins targeting one specific student, flooding their personal inbox with abusive direct messages and posting derogatory, offensive comments in public campus Discord servers.
3. **The Amplification:** The aggressors create anonymous, fake Instagram accounts dedicated entirely to sharing humiliating memes about the victim's appearance or personal life, actively encouraging other batchmates to join the harassment.
4. **The Doxing:** The situation crosses into dangerous territory when the group publishes the victim's private phone number and hostel room details online, inviting strangers to harass them offline.

[cite_start]**Who is usually targeted:**  College students who are highly active in online communities, those who express minority opinions, or individuals perceived as socially vulnerable are frequent targets. The veil of digital anonymity heavily emboldens the perpetrators.

[cite_start]**The consequences:**  The aftermath for the victim is deeply destructive. Psychologically, they often suffer from severe anxiety, panic attacks, and depression, leading to a sharp decline in academic attendance and performance. Socially, victims are forced to isolate themselves and delete their digital footprint. When doxing is involved, the distress is compounded by genuine physical safety threats, often necessitating immediate intervention from university disciplinary committees and local law enforcement.
